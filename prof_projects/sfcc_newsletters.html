San Francisco Chamber of Commerce (SFCC) Marketing Newsletters

Marketing Performance Analysis & Content Development

Overview

I contributed to the development and optimization of email newsletters for the San Francisco Chamber of Commerce, with a focus on member outreach, event promotion, and community engagement. My work spanned content creation, data-driven performance analysis, and cross-functional collaboration with multiple internal teams.

Rebranding & Content Development

I joined SFCC during a major rebranding effort and worked closely with the marketing team to ensure all newsletters reflected the new brand identity, maintaining consistency in tone, layout, and messaging across communications.

  • Newsletters were aligned with updated branding guidelines.
  • Close collaboration with the marketing team helped maintain consistency across all materials.
  • My contributions supported the transition toward a more cohesive brand presence.

Newsletter Content Analysis & Optimization

Working directly with newsletter content, I used engagement insights to guide improvements in layout and content prioritization.

  • Engagement patterns were analyzed using heatmaps.
  • High-performing sections and drop-off points were identified to inform layout decisions.
  • Content placement was optimized to increase visibility and interaction.

Event Promotion & Strategy

Heatmap data revealed where readers' attention was concentrated within each newsletter, and I leveraged those insights to strategically place high-priority events in the highest-engagement zones, ensuring key events received maximum visibility rather than being buried in low-traffic sections.

  • Heatmap hotspots were cross-referenced with event placement to determine optimal positioning.
  • High-priority events were highlighted based on engagement data.
  • Optimized placement improved the visibility of key initiatives.
  • Repositioned events were tracked to measure lift in click-through engagement.

Cross-Functional Collaboration

Working across multiple teams, I helped support broader organizational goals, including promoting small businesses and internal initiatives such as Yes SF and the San Francisco Center for Economic Development.

  • Cross-team collaboration kept messaging and outreach aligned.
  • My work supported initiatives focused on local business growth and economic development.
  • Targeted communication helped increase awareness of SFCC programs.
