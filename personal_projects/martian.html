Case Study Competition: Martian Farming Initiative

Data Analysis & Predictive Modeling Project

Project Overview

This project focused on developing a data-driven approach to support NASA's goal of building a self-sustaining farm on Mars. The objective was to identify optimal crops and locations based on environmental conditions and resource availability.

  • Analyzed how temperature, water availability, and location impact crop viability
  • Used data to guide agricultural decision-making in extreme environments

Data & Analytical Approach

The dataset included environmental and agricultural variables used to model crop performance under Martian conditions.

Key variables:

  • Temperature, sunlight exposure, and water availability
  • Crop yield predictions and risk factors

Analytical techniques applied:

  • Regression to predict crop yield
  • Clustering (K-means) to identify optimal farming zones
  • Scatter plots to visualize relationships between variables

Environmental Analysis

We evaluated how environmental factors influence crop growth and overall yield potential.

  • Identified conditions where crops achieve highest average yields
  • Compared how temperature and water availability interact
  • Determined which crops perform best under specific environmental constraints

Yield Modeling & Insights

Regression analysis was used to compare actual vs. predicted crop yields and evaluate model accuracy.

  • Found that colder temperatures improve prediction accuracy
  • Optimal yield conditions occur near -66°C
  • Strong alignment between predicted and actual yields indicates reliable model performance

Water Availability Impact

Water availability was a key driver of crop yield performance across all models.

  • Higher water availability correlates with increased crop yields
  • Data points with more water availability align closer to ideal prediction outcomes
  • Confirms water as a critical limiting resource for Martian agriculture

Optimal Farming Zones (Clustering Analysis)

Clustering techniques were used to identify the most suitable geographic regions for farming.

Best conditions found in areas with:

  • High water availability
  • Moderate temperatures (-60°C to -65°C)

Top locations identified:

  • Gale Crater
  • Valles Marineris Basin

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, we identified optimal strategies for crop selection and farm placement.

Location Strategy:

  • Prioritize regions with high water availability
  • Target temperature range of -60°C to -65°C

Crop Selection:

  • Red Planet Rice (highest yield potential)
  • Mars Soybeans (strong performance under current conditions)
  • Martian Wheat (water-efficient option)

Key Insights

This project demonstrates how data science can be applied to solve complex, real-world problems in extreme environments.

  • Crop success depends heavily on environmental constraints and resource optimization
  • Combining regression and clustering enables more informed decision-making
  • Data-driven approaches can support sustainable agriculture beyond Earth
